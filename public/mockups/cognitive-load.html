Progressive Abstraction
Full tree visible, fidelity decreases with distance from focus
The full tree is always visible, but cards decrease in fidelity based on distance from the focused node. The focused node (Staffing Gap) and its children show full detail. The parent (Raj) is compact. Siblings (Compliance, Knowledge Transfer) are compact with reduced opacity. Grandchildren (Lisa's impacts) appear as small dots. A minimap in the corner shows the overall tree shape. Tradeoffs: You always see the full tree shape and can orient yourself spatially. But the canvas gets physically large with deep trees, and distant nodes are too small to read.

Stacking / Deck
Resolved / deferred branches collapse into stacked summary cards
Only the active exploration is shown prominently on the canvas. Resolved branches (Compliance) get "closed" into a compact summary card that visually stacks/tucks to a sidebar. Deferred branches (Knowledge Transfer) also stack away. A Completed / Deferred dock at the bottom holds the stacked cards. The canvas stays clean because only active work is visible. Tradeoffs: Very clean active workspace. But completed work is out of sight and you might lose awareness of the full picture.

Swimlanes
Each impact domain gets its own vertical lane — depth goes downward
Each impact domain (Compliance, Staffing, Knowledge Transfer) gets its own vertical lane. Depth grows downward within each lane. Only one lane is expanded at a time (Staffing). Collapsed lanes show a header + one-line summary. Lane headers are always visible so you can see all domains at a glance. Tradeoffs: Great for parallel domain awareness. You always see how many branches exist and their status. But deep single branches push the page down, and cross-branch relationships are harder to show.
